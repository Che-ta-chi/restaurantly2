Our Pride is in the taste

Moi Moi is a steamed bean pudding made from ground peeled beans, onions, and a variety of seasonings. It can be served as a side dish or a main course and is often garnished with boiled eggs and vegetables.

Nigerian Jellof Rice

Jollof Rice is often considered Nigeria's signature dish. It's a one-pot rice dish cooked with tomatoes, peppers, onions, and a blend of spices. Variations of Jollof Rice can include chicken, beef, or seafood. The rice is known for its vibrant red color and delicious smoky flavor.

Beyound the Taste

Egusi Soup is a rich and hearty Nigerian soup made with ground melon seeds, leafy vegetables, and a variety of meats or fish. It's typically thickened with palm oil and served with fufu, a starchy side dish made from cassava, yams, or plantain.

Akara often known as bean cake is a classic Nigerian fritta made from bean flour.

it is bite-sized, savory, crispy, and perfect for breakfast

Akara is a popular Nigerian breakfast dish. These deep-fried bean cakes are made from black-eyed peas and seasoned with spices like onions and chili peppers. Akara is often served with pap (a porridge made from maize), bread, or fried yam.

Suya is a traditional smoked spiced meat skewer.

Suya is a popular Nigerian street food. It consists of skewered and grilled meat (usually beef, chicken, or goat) that's marinated in a flavorful peanut sauce. Suya is often served with spicy pepper sauce, onions, and tomatoes.
